Pokemon Go

**Pokémon GO** is a popular augmented reality (AR) mobile game developed by Niantic in collaboration with Nintendo and The Pokémon Company. Released in July 2016, it allows players to explore real-world environments using GPS and AR technology to find and capture virtual Pokémon that appear on their phones. Players walk around real locations to discover and catch Pokémon, visit PokéStops to collect items, and battle at Gyms for team dominance. Players choose from three teams—Mystic, Valor, or Instinct—and work together to compete for control of Gyms. Special events, such as Raid Battles and Community Days, offer opportunities for cooperation, rare Pokémon, and rewards. The game also features Trainer Battles, a GO Battle League, and the ability to add friends, trade Pokémon, and send gifts, encouraging both social interaction and physical activity while exploring the world around them.
